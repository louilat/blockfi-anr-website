Systemic Risk

Modeling Systemic Risks for Stablecoins

Stablecoins are a pivotal innovation in the realm of cryptocurrencies because they provide a dependable medium of exchange that bridges the gap between traditional and decentralized finance. The stability of these coins is achieved through various mechanisms, including collateralization, algorithmic control, and in some instances, a combination of both. These mechanisms, while promising, are far from infallible, as demonstrated by the catastrophic failure of Terra’s stablecoin. In this ANR project, we will focus on algorithmic stabilization mechanisms, studying the conditions under which they exhibit true resilience to market shocks and herd behaviors.

Modeling Systemic Risks in Lending Protocols

Decentralized lending protocols enable their users to borrow without the help of financial intermediaries. These DeFi lending platforms leverage smart contracts, i.e., self-executing agreements with the contractual terms between buyer and seller directly written into code. To ensure the security of their loans, DeFi lending platforms require borrowers to provide collateral. Borrowers lock up a specified amount of cryptocurrency, and in the event of repayment failure, the collateral is liquidated to cover the debt. Contagion arises when liquidations trigger a cascade of failures. Popular protocols include Compound, Aave, MakerDAO, Yearn Finance. Despite their initial success, there remains a lack of comprehensive understanding regarding the specific risks and optimal design principles of DeFi lending platforms. We will explore these two dimensions and outline new methodologies for the extraction and monitoring of the network of financial liabilities connecting DeFi users.

Automated Market Makers (AMM)

Mechanisms of AMM

AMMs are governed by mathematical formulas relating the amounts of tokens available in their pools, such as the constant product formula that constrains the product of the reserve assets to remain constant. Recent AMMs support different functionalities such as liquidity concentration (supported by Uniswap V3, Curve V2, TraderJoe V2.1), types of tokens available in a pool (Balancer offers up to 16 tokens), or the way fees are computed for liquidity providers. This WP will build an unambiguous knowledge base of AMM functionalities starting from their open-source codes and representing them in a formal and abstract way using formal methods (WP 3).

Probabilistic Modeling of Impermanent Loss and Fees Rewards

For some constant market marking functions (CMMF), the difference between the value of the liquidity provided to a pool and the one of the liquidity left outside is negative (a.k.a. Impermanent Loss). This loss is presumably compensated by the fees collected by liquidity providers. The mathematical modeling and analysis of Impermanent Loss and related fees is challenging when all AMM mechanisms are taken into account. This will be done in conjunction with WP 4, to measure liquidity risk and market depth. It is important to study the trade-off between operational risk and liquidity risk in such choices.

Formal Methods

Formalization

We will formalize the behavior of several main smart contract classes that can be found in DeFi applications, such as Automated Market Makers, Lending and Borrowing platforms, or algorithmic stablecoins. To this aim, we will use abstract languages based on first-order logic, where transactions could be denoted for instance as Horn clauses or deduction rules. Non-classical logics will also be considered: dynamic logic, probabilistic logic, or separation logic.

Automated Reasoning Tools

We will develop automated deduction tools for analyzing the behavior of the protocols under consideration and identifying their properties. We will use abductive reasoning to automatically derive conditions that can cause the unwanted behavior of these protocols.

Statistical Methods for Data Analysis of DeFi Data and Related Risks

Data Collection and Preprocessing

In this initial phase, we will collect and preprocess relevant DeFi transaction data, including timestamps, transaction volumes, and cryptoasset prices using robust statistics.

Statistical Models

We will develop advanced statistical models to analyze the DeFi ecosystem, including the application of self-exciting point processes like Hawkes models to capture transaction intensity, latent factors driving transaction activity, and their impact on transaction frequency.

Bayesian Algorithms for Model Calibration

We will use sophisticated Bayesian algorithms, specifically Sequential Monte Carlo (SMC) and Approximate Bayesian Computation (ABC), to estimate the parameters of the models. Bayesian methods enable the simultaneous quantification of uncertainty around the model and its parameters, making them a powerful tool for financial risk management.
